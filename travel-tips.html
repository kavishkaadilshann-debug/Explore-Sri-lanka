Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, most international travelers need an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) or tourist visa to visit Sri Lanka. The application process is quick and can usually be completed online through the official Sri Lankan government portal. Visa requirements may vary depending on your nationality, so it is best to check the latest requirements before you travel.
Sri Lanka is a year-round destination, but the best time depends on the region. For the south and west coasts (including Galle and Colombo) and the hill country (Ella), December to April is ideal. For the east coast (Trincomalee and Arugam Bay), May to September is best.
The official currency is the Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). Credit and debit cards (Visa/Mastercard) are widely accepted in hotels, major restaurants, and supermarkets. However, it is highly recommended to carry cash for small local shops, street food, tuk-tuks, and entry tickets.
Yes, Sri Lanka has multiple travel options. For short distances, local tuk-tuks are convenient and fun. For longer journeys, trains offer scenic views (especially the Kandy-to-Ella route). Private drivers are also popular, affordable, and offer flexibility for tourists.
Traditional Rice and Curry, Kottu Roti, and Hoppers can be spicy! Don't hesitate to ask restaurants for "mild spice" if you are sensitive. Drink fresh King Coconut water (Thambili) to cool down.
